Mathematics

Math 112

This mathematics course is designed for students with some prior knowledge of calculus from high school (AP Calculus AB/BC). This class has a main focus on differential calculus with topics of continuity, limits, and differentiation.

Math 115

This mathematics course is designed for students with prior knowledge of calculus from high school (AP Calculus AB/BC). This class is focused on integral calculus and heuristics such as Riemann sums, approximations, and series and their divergence/convergence. There are weekly problem sets, two midterms, and a final exam.

Math 120

This mathematics course is designed for students who are well prepared in both differential and integral calculus. This class is focused on multivariable calculus, and expanding ideas of differentiating and integrating over higher-dimensional domains. This class is known to be difficult during midterms and finals due to the wide scope of content covered.

Math 116/121

This sequence is intended for students studying biology. The materials and mathematical methods covered in this class have direct applications to biological phenomena. There are weekly problem sets, one midterm, and a final exam. There are weekly readings that demonstrate mathematical models in a biological context.

Math 230/231

This class is meant for students who are well prepared in mathematics and intend to pursue higher level mathematics courses or the mathematics major. Topics include multivariable calculus, linear algebra, vector analysis, and more.
